A note on entity naming  |  RocketSource recommendation

Throughout this document, the program is referred to consistently as “Costco Elavon Payment Processing” — a deliberate GEO strategy, not a brand preference. We wanted to explain the rationale and flag it as a recommendation open to discussion.

Why “Costco Elavon Payment Processing” and not just “Elavon”

Large language models and AI Overview systems resolve entities through co-occurrence — they learn what a name means based on how it appears in context across the web. “Elavon” as a standalone entity already carries its own associations in AI training data: US Bank’s payment processor, with its own reviews, pricing, and corporate context entirely separate from the Costco member program.

When an AI sees “Elavon offers no cancellation fee,” it correctly attributes that fact to Elavon-the-company. The Costco landing page earns no credit. By contrast, “Costco Elavon Payment Processing” forces AI systems to treat the co-branded program as a distinct named entity — with its own features, pricing tiers, and membership eligibility. That distinction is what causes an AI to cite Costco’s page specifically when a user asks “what payment processing does Costco offer?”

There is also a direct query-matching benefit. Real users searching this category type “Costco payment processing,” “Costco Elavon rates,” and “Costco merchant services” — not just “Elavon.” Embedding the compound entity name in every FAQ answer creates semantic alignment with those exact queries.

How we apply this in the FAQs

The first sentence of every FAQ answer — the sentence most likely to be extracted as a standalone chunk by an AI — always carries the full “Costco Elavon Payment Processing” or “Costco Elavon payment processing” construction. Subsequent sentences may use “Elavon payment processing” or “the program” for readability. The entity is planted where it matters most.

RocketSource recommendation

This naming convention is our strategic recommendation based on GEO best practices. If the Costco or Elavon brand teams have a preferred program name — or if existing brand guidelines specify how the co-branded program should be referred to in external content — we are happy to align to that language and update all 10 FAQs accordingly. The underlying structure and GEO strategy remain the same regardless of the entity name used.
